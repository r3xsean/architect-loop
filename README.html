Sheet 2 — Organizing idea

Every bad guess is an unanswered question

The idea comes from Thariq (@trq212): agents do bad work when they have to guess, and every guess traces back to an unknown. The loop finds the questions cheaply — before wrong guesses get expensive — routes each kind of question to the right tool, and gates the merge on you actually understanding what was built.

It works in both directions. The blindspot pass doesn't just convert your unknown unknowns into questions Claude asks — its digest teaches them back to you: the traps ahead, the quality bars you may not know exist, the vocabulary you'll need. The person answering the one-way doors is calibrated before the first question lands. Taste questions follow the same rule: variants to react to if you can judge the domain, a short teach-first explainer if you can't.

Every explainer, brainstorm, interview, prototype, and reference is a cheap way to find out what you didn't know — before it gets expensive to fix. — Thariq, “A Field Guide to Fable: Finding Your Unknowns”

Sheet 3 — Architecture

Two primary sessions, one loop

Fable high runs architect and Blueprint. After approval, a fresh Sol-xhigh session completes the entire code spec. Each delegates bounded work and verification crosses families.

Claude Fable

decides and judges
  • The architect interview and blueprint approval
  • Prose, positioning, and taste-led production
  • Experiential seams and cross-family review
  • Medium normally; high for consequential judgment
each reviews the other's work

Codex / GPT-5.6

executes, delegates & verifies
  • Sol xhigh completes the whole approved code spec
  • Sol medium handles research and normal checked production
  • Luna xhigh handles bounded leaves that Sol checks
  • Sol owns visual verification and final aggregation
The generator never grades its own work.

Sol- or Luna-made normal work is reviewed by Fable medium; consequential work by Fable high. Fable-made normal work is reviewed by Sol medium; consequential work by Sol xhigh. Reviewers get the acceptance contract, sources, and actual diff or artifact — never the builder's Implementation Notes.

Review effort scales with blast radius

The blueprint records how much damage the work could do; review depth follows.

Low

Standards + Spec axes, cross-model, with auto-fix and delta-check verification. The default for contained changes.

Medium

Everything at low, plus a human-authored acceptance contract — invariants and anti-requirements in plain language: “X never happens; data never leaves Y.”

High

Auth, money, user data, destructive actions, external side effects. Adds the Tail axis — “does it do anything extra?” — contract-derived probes the builder never saw, and a final all-axes re-sweep.

Findings are auto-fixed by the builder and re-verified cross-model, up to three rounds. The human is pulled in for exactly two things: a finding that survives every round, or a fix that would change an approved plan decision.
